John Free : Photographer

Street and social documentary photographer

Background

John Free is a social documentary/street photographer who lives in Los Angeles. His photographic essays range from railroad tramps in California to automobile abstracts to London and Paris street life.

Since about 40 years ago, John Free has been photographing people for different companies, museums, and even non-profit organizations.

John's "End of the line" project is probably his most famous work. This project took place in the Los Angeles freight yards, where he photographed people known as railroad "Tramps" for ten years.

Street Photography

John Free's personal work involves street photography. His style is influenced by the likes of Robert Frank, Henri Cartier-Bresson and Eugene Smith. He believes in getting close and capturing the lives of every day people in a photograph.

Workshops

For the past 20 years, John Free has been conducting workshops teaching street photography in various locations around the world, including New York, Los Angeles, and London. He believes that learning street photography can help people improve themselves in the other photographic disciplines: portraiture, wedding, and photojournalism to name a few.

How John Free has influenced me

I have been a photographer for several years now. John Free was one fo the first photographers that I followed early on in my journey.

I beleive that street photography is one of the best ways to document life as it happens in the moment. It's also the best way to document history in the making. I try to catch people in all of their candid moments, and I hope to convey some kind of emotion out of people who view my photographic works.
